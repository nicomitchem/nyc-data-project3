Graffiti arguably got its start in Manhattan in the late 1960s and early 1970s, when writers like TAKI 183 began tagging their names all over the city. This led to a wave of graffiti that spread throughout the borough and eventually beyond. In the 1980s, Manhattan graffiti became more stylized and artistic, with artists like KEITH HARING and JEAN-MICHEL BASQUIAT gaining fame for their work.
